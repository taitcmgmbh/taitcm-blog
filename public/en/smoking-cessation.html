Master Smoking Cessation Naturally – Your Path to Freedom with TCM

1. How does TCM understand smoking cessation?

Smoking addiction is understood as a craving for heat, lack of air, and psychological tension. The goal is to regulate body and mind simultaneously and lower the "heat of craving".

2. Does acupuncture really help with quitting?

Yes. Especially ear acupuncture (NADA protocol) can significantly reduce cravings, soothe nervousness, and weaken withdrawal symptoms such as sleep disorders or irritability.

3. What do herbs contribute?

They detoxify the lungs, clear phlegm, calm the heart, and promote elimination. Also highly recommended for people with coughs, shortness of breath, or inner restlessness.

4. Who is the method suitable for?

For everyone who wants to quit naturally without nicotine replacement therapy – with sustainable effects, without weight gain or inner restlessness.

5. How long does the treatment take?

Ideally 2–3 sessions per week for 3–6 weeks. Additionally important: stress reduction, motivation, exercise, clear goals – then the path to freedom is realistic.
