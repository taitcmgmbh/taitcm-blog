Natural Relief for Arthritis & Osteoarthritis – The TCM Approach

1. How does TCM understand arthritis?

In TCM, arthritis is classified as a "Bi syndrome"—caused by external pathogens such as wind, cold, dampness, or heat, combined with internal weakness. Symptoms include pain, swelling, and movement restrictions, which often fluctuate.

2. How does acupuncture work for arthritis?

Acupuncture can relieve pain, inhibit inflammation, and improve mobility—it is particularly effective in chronic phases. Heat applications such as moxibustion can further enhance this effect.

3. Are herbs suitable for every form?

Therapy depends on the individual pattern: for cold and dampness, herbs have warming and draining effects; for heat, they are cooling and anti-inflammatory; for blood stagnation, they promote circulation. An individual diagnosis is essential for effective treatment.

4. Can TCM be combined with Western therapy?

Yes. TCM complements conventional medical treatments very well—it can mitigate side effects and strengthen constitutional stability.

5. Is long-term treatment useful?

Absolutely. For chronic arthritis, TCM aims for relapse prevention, function improvement, and enhanced quality of life through regular acupuncture, herbs, and lifestyle care.
