Holistic Relief for Headaches – The TCM Approach

1. How does TCM view headaches?

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, headaches are not seen merely as symptoms, but as expressions of internal imbalance. Common causes include:

  • Liver Qi Stagnation (often stress-related)
  • Blood Deficiency
  • Wind-Cold Invasion
  • Dampness or Phlegm in the head area

The location and quality of the pain provide vital clues to the underlying disharmony.

2. Which types of headaches does TCM treat?

TCM can alleviate many types of headaches, including:

  • Tension Headaches: Usually bilateral, dull, and triggered by stress.
  • Migraines: Unilateral, throbbing, and often related to hormonal changes.
  • Weather-sensitive or cyclic headaches: Such as those occurring before menstruation or during weather shifts.

3. How is the treatment carried out?

Following a detailed diagnosis (tongue, pulse, and history), an individual therapy plan is created. This includes:

  • Acupuncture: Clears blockages and promotes the free flow of Qi.
  • Tuina Massage: Especially effective for muscle tension in the neck and shoulder area.
  • Chinese Herbs: For nourishing blood or specifically regulating Qi.

4. How quickly does TCM work for headaches?

For acute tension headaches, even a single treatment can bring noticeable relief. For chronic migraines, regular therapy over several weeks or months is advisable. Our goal is long-term stabilization of your system, not just short-term symptom suppression.
