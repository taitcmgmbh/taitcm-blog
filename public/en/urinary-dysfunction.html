Natural UTI Relief – The TCM Solution

1. How does TCM view Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs)?

TCM understands recurring bladder infections as a sign of internal imbalance—mostly caused by Damp-Heat in the lower burner or a weakness of the Spleen and Kidneys.

2. Does acupuncture help with bladder complaints?

Yes. Acupuncture regulates bladder function, reduces inflammation, and alleviates symptoms such as burning, urgency, and pressure. Especially suitable for overactive bladder or chronic conditions.

3. Are herbs an alternative to antibiotics?

For mild or recurring complaints, yes—they have anti-inflammatory, strengthening, and expelling effects. For severe infections, we recommend a combination with Western medicine.

4. How can TCM help in prevention?

TCM strengthens the constitution, promotes warmth in the lower abdomen, and helps to expel dampness. It is a great complement to Western medicine, especially for women with frequent infections.

5. How long does the treatment take?

Acute complaints often improve within a few days. For constitutional stabilization, we recommend 4–6 weeks of follow-up treatment with herbs and lifestyle counseling.
