Infertility & Fertility Support – The Natural Way with TCM

1. How does TCM view infertility in women?

TCM distinguishes patterns such as Kidney-Yang deficiency, Liver-Qi stagnation, Phlegm-Dampness, or Blood deficiency. The goal is to naturally regulate the cycle, hormones, and uterine lining.

2. What are the benefits of acupuncture for fertility?

Acupuncture improves blood circulation in the pelvis, reduces stress, regulates hormones, and promotes ovulation – often combined with cycle observation.

3. How do Chinese herbs work for conception?

Depending on the cycle phase, they support egg maturation, ovulation, the luteal phase, and implantation – individually tailored, even for PCOS or endometriosis.

4. Support during IVF/IUI – is it possible?

Yes. TCM accompanies every phase: preparation before stimulation, mitigation of side effects, and promoting implantation after embryo transfer – scientifically supported.

5. How long should one prepare?

Ideally 3 months or longer to optimally prepare the body. Depending on the starting situation, shorter or longer support is also possible.
