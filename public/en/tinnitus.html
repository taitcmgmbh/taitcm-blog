Natural Tinnitus Treatment – Finding Your Inner Quiet

1. How does TCM view Tinnitus?

According to TCM, tinnitus often results from an imbalance in the Kidneys and Liver – e.g., Kidney Yin deficiency, rising Liver Fire, or phlegm blockages. Stress, overload, and sensory overstimulation are also considered triggers.

2. Does acupuncture help with ear ringing?

Yes. Acupuncture can improve blood circulation in the ear, calm the nervous system, and harmonize the internal energy pathways. It is particularly helpful for acute or stress-related tinnitus.

3. Are herbs necessary?

For chronic or constitutionally related tinnitus, individually tailored herbs support the treatment – for example, by strengthening the Kidneys or reducing internal heat.

4. Is early treatment important?

Absolutely. The earlier the therapy begins, the higher the chances of success. Chronic cases require more patience.

5. Does TCM have side effects?

The procedures are gentle, well-tolerated, and individually adaptable. Ideal for patients seeking a natural alternative or who do not tolerate medication.
