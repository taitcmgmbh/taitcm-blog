Hay Fever Treatment in Zurich – Acupuncture & TCM

Hay fever (pollen allergy) can cause sneezing, itching, runny or blocked nose and irritated eyes. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) offers a natural and holistic treatment using acupuncture and herbal therapy to relieve symptoms and strengthen the immune system long-term.

1. How does TCM understand hay fever?

In TCM, hay fever is often seen as a weakness of the body's defensive energy (Wei Qi), combined with internal imbalance such as Lung Qi deficiency or dampness. This makes the body more sensitive to external allergens.

2. Does acupuncture help with symptoms?

Yes. Acupuncture can open the nasal passages, reduce itching, calm inflammation and improve mucosal function. It is especially helpful during the pollen season.

3. Can Chinese herbs help long-term?

Herbal therapy can regulate the immune system, strengthen the respiratory tract and reduce allergic reactions. The goal is long-term stability and improved tolerance.

4. When should treatment start?

Ideally 1–2 months before the pollen season begins. Early treatment can significantly reduce the intensity and frequency of symptoms.

5. Is TCM safe for allergies?

Yes. TCM is a gentle and well-tolerated therapy. It is especially suitable for patients who experience side effects from antihistamines or prefer a natural approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can TCM cure hay fever?
The goal is to reduce symptoms and improve tolerance. Results vary individually.

In many cases yes, but individual consultation is recommended.
