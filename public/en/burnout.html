Burnout Treatment in Zurich – Acupuncture & TCM

Burnout often shows itself through deep exhaustion, inner restlessness, sleep problems and the feeling of no longer being able to recover properly. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) supports body and mind with acupuncture, herbal therapy and holistic regulation to rebuild energy, improve sleep and restore inner stability step by step.

1. How does TCM understand burnout?

In TCM, burnout is often understood as an exhaustion of Qi, Blood and Jing. Common patterns include Spleen Qi weakness, Liver Qi stagnation, Heart Blood deficiency or a weakness of Kidney energy. The body is overloaded and no longer regenerates sufficiently.

2. Can acupuncture help with exhaustion and stress?

Yes. Acupuncture calms the autonomic nervous system, reduces inner tension, supports better sleep and helps restore the flow of Qi. Many patients experience the treatment as deeply relaxing and stabilizing.

3. What role do Chinese herbs play?

Chinese herbs are prescribed individually. They may strengthen Qi and Blood, calm the mind, support digestion and improve recovery. Unlike stimulants, the goal is not short-term activation but sustainable rebuilding.

4. Is TCM a substitute for psychotherapy?

No. In cases of severe burnout, depression or strong psychological distress, medical or psychological care may be important. TCM can be a valuable complement because it supports physical exhaustion, sleep and nervous system regulation.

5. How long does recovery take?

Recovery depends on the depth and duration of exhaustion. Some patients feel calmer and sleep better after only a few treatments, while deeper exhaustion often requires several weeks or months of continuous support.
